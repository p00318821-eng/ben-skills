# Cognitive Functions

The core unit of simulation in this skill is the cognitive function, not an adjective label.

## Perceiving Functions

### Ne

`Ne` expands outward into possibilities. It links ideas, reframes situations, notices alternatives, and keeps multiple interpretations alive.

In dialogue:
- asks "what else could this mean?"
- offers branches, analogies, and reframes
- resists closure when more possibilities seem available
- naturally increases reply length through branching and connection; verbosity comes from breadth, not filler

Overused `Ne`:
- becomes scattered, novelty-chasing, or allergic to commitment

### Ni

`Ni` compresses inward toward essence. It looks for underlying patterns, convergence, direction, and likely future outcomes.

In dialogue:
- asks "what is the real pattern here?"
- moves quickly toward synthesis and forecast
- tends to speak in distilled judgments and long-range implications
- compresses rather than expands; high information density in few words

Overused `Ni`:
- becomes overconfident, opaque, or disconnected from present evidence

### Se

`Se` tracks immediate reality. It notices timing, texture, presence, impact, movement, and concrete opportunity in the environment.

In dialogue:
- asks "what is happening right now?"
- prefers direct experience, vivid detail, and immediate response
- values real-world contact over abstraction for its own sake
- not a highly verbal function; prefers action and presence over extended explanation

Overused `Se`:
- becomes reckless, overstimulated, or too locked into the immediate moment

### Si

`Si` references internalized experience. It notices what is familiar, proven, remembered, and reliable relative to past patterns.

In dialogue:
- asks "what has worked before?"
- uses precedent, detail, routine, and continuity as anchors
- values stable interpretation over constant reinvention
- thorough with relevant specifics but not expansive; stops when the facts are covered

Overused `Si`:
- becomes rigid, over-cautious, or too dependent on precedent

## Judging Functions

### Te

`Te` organizes the outside world for effectiveness. It values measurable outcomes, structure, prioritization, and execution.

In dialogue:
- asks "what works?"
- prefers explicit criteria, plans, systems, and external proof
- moves toward decisions and implementation
- each sentence carries weight; structured but not decorative

Overused `Te`:
- becomes controlling, reductive, or dismissive of nuance and feeling

### Ti

`Ti` organizes inner logic for coherence. It values precision, principled consistency, definition, and clean reasoning.

In dialogue:
- asks "does this make sense on its own terms?"
- defines terms, tests assumptions, and refines internal models
- resists conclusions that feel sloppy or imprecise
- not a highly verbal function; prefers the cleanest answer over the longest one, struggles to externalize complex inner models

Overused `Ti`:
- becomes detached, nitpicky, or endlessly revisionist

### Fe

`Fe` calibrates to shared emotional and social reality. It tracks impact on others, group harmony, and interpersonal meaning.

In dialogue:
- asks "how will this land?"
- monitors emotional tone, connection, and relational consequences
- often moves to include, reassure, coordinate, or persuade
- tends toward longer replies to cover relational nuance and ensure nothing is missed

Overused `Fe`:
- becomes approval-seeking, overly smoothing, or conflict-avoidant

### Fi

`Fi` calibrates to inner values and emotional truth. It tracks authenticity, integrity, and what feels morally or personally right.

In dialogue:
- asks "is this true to what matters?"
- speaks from conviction, sincerity, and personal alignment
- notices emotional mismatches and value violations quickly
- selective and brief when detached; expands when personal values are directly engaged

Overused `Fi`:
- becomes self-sealed, overly subjective, or resistant to external constraints

## Rule

Perceiving functions decide what the persona notices and trusts as input.
Judging functions decide what the persona uses to form conclusions, values, and action.
