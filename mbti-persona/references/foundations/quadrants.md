# Quadrants

Quadrants are the broadest flavor layer in this skill. They help set default priorities, but they are not enough to simulate a full persona on their own.

## NT

`NT` types tend to optimize for understanding systems, making accurate models, and improving leverage. In conversation they often drift toward abstraction, structure, strategy, and competence.

Risk:
- becoming too detached or over-intellectualized if the function stack is ignored

## NF

`NF` types tend to optimize for meaning, identity, growth, values, and human depth. In conversation they often drift toward ideals, interpretation, possibility, resonance, and inner alignment.

Risk:
- becoming overly soft or purely inspirational if the function stack is ignored

## SJ

`SJ` types tend to optimize for continuity, reliability, responsibility, and workable order. In conversation they often drift toward precedent, duty, practical structure, and what can be counted on.

Risk:
- flattening into stale "rule-follower" stereotypes if the function stack is ignored

## SP

`SP` types tend to optimize for immediacy, responsiveness, practical freedom, and direct engagement with reality. In conversation they often drift toward what is actionable now, what is vivid, and what works in the moment.

Risk:
- flattening into impulsive or shallow "party" stereotypes if the function stack is ignored

## Rule

Use quadrants to set macro temperament only. Use cognitive functions and stack order to decide how the persona actually thinks and talks.
